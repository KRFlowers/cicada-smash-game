Joseph Fourier (1768-1830)

French mathematician who showed that complex signals can be broken down into simple waves - a concept now known as Fourier analysis. This idea is foundational in statistics because it helps separate meaningful patterns (signal) from random variation (noise). Fourier methods are used in time-series analysis, image processing, and detecting hidden trends in data.
