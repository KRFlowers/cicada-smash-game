Joseph Fourier (1768–1830)

Joseph Fourier (1768–1830) was a French mathematician who demonstrated that complex signals can be expressed as combinations of simple waves, a principle now known as Fourier analysis.

This idea is important in statistics because it provides a framework for distinguishing systematic patterns (signal) from random variation (noise) in data.

Fourier methods are widely used in time-series analysis, image processing, and other applications where identifying underlying structure in data is essential.
